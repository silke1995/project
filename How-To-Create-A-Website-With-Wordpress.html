Before We Begin Explained Below are a few definitions you need to know to strengthen your knowledge in what we are about to learn.(PS Feel Free to Skip )

A . What Is A Website?

The definition of a website is a page or collection of pages on the World Wide Web that contains specific information which was all provided by one person or entity and traces back to a common Uniform Resource Locator (URL). Facebook , MySpace , and Hilax Media are examples of websites.

B . Popular Types of Websites You Can Create with Wordpress?

  • E-commerce Website. An e-commerce website is a website people can directly buy products from.
  • Business Website.
  • Entertainment Website.
  • Portfolio Website.
  • Media Website.
  • Brochure Website.
  • Nonprofit Website.

C. Why Creating a website is important?

A well-maintained website can help you gain a competitive edge in your business and enhance your business image. Developing your web site offers many advantages including helping you get more leads and possibilities, boost sales, improve your professional brand, and improve your customer service.

D. What is the distinction between a webpage and website?

The distinction between a website and a web page is that a website is a collection of web pages with information on a subject, and a web page is a smaller part of a larger website usually containing more specific information. If a website were a book, then a webpage would be a chapter in that book.

Here’s Why Most People Create a Website with WordPress.

Unlike website builders, it’s completely free. If you’re new to website design, I recommend using WordPress because WordPress is the easiest platform to work with, and it’s flexible enough to suit everyone – small business websites, online shops, bigger organizations and so on. It’s the easiest to use and often faster when creating a new website. Get More Insight About Wordpress

.

But more importantly:

You don’t need to enter any coding to get WordPress to do what you want. Through the use of thousands of free (and paid) themes and plugins, you can quickly create the perfect site in mere instants. However, it’s also easy to customize if you have coding knowledge. It’s perfect for users of all skill levels.

Advantages of Creating a Website Wordpress.

1. Code-Free Content Management. The biggest benefit of WordPress is that it can almost eliminate code from the day-to-day management of your website.
2.Easy to Change How Your Site Looks.
Every WordPress site needs something called a theme. Your theme defines how your site looks on the frontend.
3.Easy to Add New Functionality WordPress also offers something called plugins that allow you to easily increase the functionality of your site.
4.Rapid Development and Changes The combination of themes and plugins, along with WordPress’ general functionality as a content management system, indicates that you can quickly spin up even complicated websites.

Disadvantages of Creating a Website Wordpress.

You’ll find some people saying things like “static HTML websites load faster” or “static HTML websites rank better in Google.” But some of this should be taken with a grain of salt. While it’s true that PHP and a database introduces additional load time and delays, it’s not that hard to get WordPress load times under one second – which is fast.

For some reason, you don’t want to build your site with WordPress, review out Drupal, Joomla and other alternative of wordpress guides as well . They are all FREE to use. But for the starters, I strongly recommend choosing to WordPress.

Don’t choose your platform right away. Most web hosting services offer one-click-installs for WordPress, Joomla, and Drupal. You can decide later and you don’t need to download/install anything.

2. Decide On A Domain Name and Web Hosting Provider.

Discovering a good web hosting provider and coming up with a good domain name which represents your business and also good for Search Engine Optimization(SEO) purposes. Both of these elements are immensely relevant if you want to build a successful website.

However, To install WordPress or any other type of website builder, it is required to have two things:

1. Domain Name (a web address like YourSiteName.com) When getting with a domain name, ask yourself: Does the domain name match the content? Is the name of your site short and good? Is the domain name easy to spell? Are you using the right domain extension, like .com or .net? Some extensions will not be accessible to you depending on where you live. Today, there is a slew of extensions accessible depending on industry and purpose. Be creative.

2.Web Hosting (a service that connects your site to the internet) Deciding on a hosting plan sets the foundation for building your site. Think of it like buying a plot of digital land to build your online structure.

3. Set up your website through the web host and Create your webiste with wordpress.

Once you’ve purchased your domain name and set up your hosting, you’re well on your way! Now it’s time to get your website operating. The first thing you’ll need to do is install WordPress to your domain. You will find types of icons to do various things on your hosting account dashboard (cPanel). Scroll down to the website section in cPanel and then click on the WordPress icon.

Click on the ‘Install Wordpress’ icon to continue. Next, you will be required to select the domain name you registered and where you want to install WordPress. Choose your domain from the dropdow n field and then click Next.

Now you’ll need to enter your website name, admin username, and a password for your website. Don’t worry, you can update them later in WordPress settings if you require to. You’ll also need to check all the checkboxes on the screen and then click on the ‘Install’ button to continue.

Quick Install will now start installing WordPress on your website. While it will show you some website templates that you can install on your site. Once the installation is over, you’ll see a success message in the top header.

Now you need to click on the ‘Installation Complete’ link, and it will take you to the screen with your WordPress login URL and password. Congratulations! You have created your first WordPress website. You can now head over to the WordPress login page. Your login URL will look like this:
http://www.yourdomain.com/wp-admin
Now You can log in to your WordPress site using the admin username and password you entered earlier.

4. Design your Website

Now let’s continue to the next step and pick a design for your website. Everything is easily defined in the WordPress dashboard. So if you’re feeling a bit confused, don’t worry – we’re going to show you where to go next.

Introduction to FREE themes

The visual appearance of your WordPress site is controlled by a WordPress theme WordPress themes are professionally designed templates that you can install on your website to change their appearance. By default, each WordPress site comes with a basic theme. If you visit your website, then it will look something like this:

Once you’re on the dashboard, you’ve got access to over 1500 free themes! Just search the sidebar for “Appearance”, then click “Themes”.

On the next screen, you will able to search from 4,000+ free WordPress themes that are available in the official WordPress.org themes directory. You can find them by popular, latest, featured, as well as other feature filters (i.e industry, layout, etc). Once you have found a theme you like, installing it is as simple as clicking “Install” followed by “Activate”.

NOTE Changing themes won’t delete your previous posts, pages, and content. You can change themes as often as you want without having to worry about losing what you’ve created.

Finish by adding content (Pages)

You’ll start by adding a few pages to your WordPress site. Don’t worry if you don’t have enough content for these pages at the moment. You can always edit and update them. Just search the sidebar for Pages » Add New page in the WordPress admin area. This is where you can add new content to your website, write articles, add pages, upload images, videos etc.



After adding content to your page, you can click on the publish button to make it live on your website. You can repeat the process by adding more pages for different sections of your website. For example, an about page, contact us, and a blog page to display blog posts.

Congratulations – You’re Ready to Launch!
